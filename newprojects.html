Intrument Management App Interface Project

This project was done in Interaction Design at Simon Fraser University.

First we wanted to identify an issue area which may be solved by the creation of a new interface. After research we landed upon the idea of an app to be used by band classes to keep track of and sort instruments.

After doing this we did extensive research into existing interfaces that are in any way similar to our domain. We saved interfaces we liked as inspiration.

We identified the persona which was the music director.

In the next stage I started with the overall UX design of the interface. This is where I took time to design various outputs so that I could present my team with design layouts for our app, this was inspired by the previous inspiration step. We came together and combined our ideas finally landing on a final design and following style guide.

Finally we started making our wireframes and fully prototyping the full app. I took on the responsibility of prototyping the tutorial which is the first thing the user sees when interacting with our app. This is a very important feature in the user experience as it plays the role of the first impression and explaining how the app is used.

Next I prototyped the adding an instrument stream. This included various steps as it was important to properly document the instrument for inventory reasons.

My team mates prototyped the other streams and we came together with our first iteration. Next we moved onto our user testing phase where we tested our app and identified issues.

After identifying the issues I went in and fixed my streams along with the other streams. Finally we had our app created with the issues ironed out.

Next we created a website to advertise our app and we presented this to our class.

Fluff & Fibre Website Project

This project was done in iat 339, my partner Rebecca Wong and I created a website for a brand called Fluff and Fiber which is a sustainable fashion forward company which specializes in organic fibers. We started by brainstorming the brand's identity and values. Then, we created personas to understand our target audience better.

The first task was to create a style guide. I designed many buttons and color pallettes and came together with my partner to create a cohesive style guide.

Next we began creating the website. I worked on the home page, product page and the checkout page. I did this with HTML and CSS, I learned a lot about responsiveness and keeping my code cohesive and efficient. I learned the importance of having organized code which is readable and explainable.

I was very satisfied with the outcome of this project and believe our website showcased our HTML and CSS coding skills.
